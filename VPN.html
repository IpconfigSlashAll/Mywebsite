What is a VPN?

A Virtual Private Network or best known for its short abbreviation as a VPN, is a program that establishes a secure, private, and encrypted connection between networking devices over the internet. This allows users to transfer data between devices via a private network, over a public internet connection. By encrypting the data VPNs protect important information from unauthorized access and malicious hackers. VPNs also allow users to browse the internet or any web anonymously preventing websites from tracking or collecting usersâ€™ data. VPNs are like real estate agents they are like an intermediary between you and the device to which you are trying to send the data to.

How Does a VPN Works?

VPNs create a secure connection which is called a tunnel between your device and the internet, encrypting the data, and sending it to a server controlled by the VPN provider. When someone connects to a VPN the user's data travels through the tunnel, which protects the data from being seen by anyone. The tunnel is like a conversation between two people. Also, when someone connects to a VPN, they are given an IP address that can be located anywhere around the world. The given IP address acts as your real IP address and if someone tries to track it, it will give them a random location and not your actual one. VPNs are like ACLs on routers, before the data goes out it gets encrypted. The encryption makes sure to hide the data that you are sending from anyone who is trying to intercept it, this works not only on private networks but also on public networks like the ones in airports.
